Five Examples of Sorting Algorithm

Insertion Sort





Insertion Sort is a simple method for sorting a list of items. Imagine you are sorting a hand of playing cards. You pick up one card at a time and insert it into its correct position in the cards you've already sorted. In the same way, Insertion Sort takes each item from the list and compares it to the items before it. It moves the item to its correct spot by shifting the other items, so the list becomes more and more sorted as you go. This process continues until every item in the list is in the right order. Insertion Sort is easy to understand and works well for small lists, but it can be slow if the list is large.
