Sorting Algorithm

What is Sorting Algorithm?

Sorting algorithm are methods used to arrange data, such as numbers or words, into a specific order (e.g., ascending or descending). These algorithms are essential for organizing and retrieving data efficiently. Sorting improves the performance of other algorithms, such as searching or merging datasets.

Five Examples of Sorting Algorithm

Bubble Sort



Bubble Sort is a simple way to sort a list of items, like numbers or words, in order from smallest to largest (or largest to smallest). It works by repeatedly going through the list and comparing two items at a time. If the first item is bigger than the second, they swap places. This process is repeated for each pair of items in the list, moving through the entire list until everything is in the right order. The reason it's called "bubble sort" is that, just like bubbles in water rise to the top, the largest items in the list "bubble up" to their correct position after each pass through the list. Even though it's easy to understand, bubble sort can be slow when there are lots of items to sort.
